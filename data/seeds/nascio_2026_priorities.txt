NASCIO State CIO Top Ten Policy and Technology Priorities for 2026

Source: National Association of State Chief Information Officers (NASCIO).
Public summary of the annual State CIO Top 10 Priorities survey (51 state and
territory CIOs). Full PDF: https://www.nascio.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/NASCIO-2026-State-Top-10-Priorities_a11y.pdf
Resource page: https://www.nascio.org/resource/state-cio-top-ten-policy-and-technology-priorities-for-2026/

This seed text is used when the live NASCIO PDF is blocked by bot-protection
from automated clients. Content reflects the publicly published 2026 list.

Headline: Artificial intelligence takes the number one spot for the first time,
overtaking cybersecurity, which held the top position for 12 straight years.
Other shifts include upward movement for accessibility and budget/cost control;
consolidation returns to the list; digital government and data management decline.

2026 Priority Technologies, Applications and Tools (Top 10):
1. Artificial Intelligence / GenAI / Agentic AI / Machine Learning
2. Application Modernization / Renovation
3. Cloud Solutions
4. Identity and Access Management
5. Software-as-a-Service (SaaS)
6. Security Enhancement Tools
7. Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP)
8. Data Management
9. Data Analytics
10. Networking

Related policy themes for state CIOs include cybersecurity and risk management,
budget and fiscal management, digital government and digital services,
identity and access management, consolidation and optimization, and accessibility.

NASCIO uses the Top 10 to shape strategic priorities, guide workgroups and issue
forums, and inform conference programming for the year ahead. State technology
leaders use this ranking as a roadmap for enterprise IT strategy across state
and local government.
